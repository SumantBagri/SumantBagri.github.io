About

Field of Work & Background

My work sits at the frontier of robotics software, computer vision, and applied AI. At Ocado Technology, I lead the development of modular 3D perception pipelines that guide autonomous robotic arms in warehouse automation — building systems that must be reliable, fast, and resilient enough to operate 24/7 in production environments. My stack spans deep learning inference (PyTorch, TensorRT), 3D spatial computation (Open3D, PhysX), high-performance services (C++, Go, gRPC, Docker), and robotics middleware (ROS2).

Before Ocado, I engineered GPU-accelerated collision detection for 6-DOF manipulators (65–70× CPU speedup), spent nearly two years at Flow Traders in Hong Kong building deterministic sub-microsecond HFT infrastructure, and built ML pipelines at Disney Interactive and IIT Bombay research labs.

I hold an MScAC in AI from the University of Toronto (4.0/4.0 GPA) and a dual B.Tech + M.Tech in Mechanical Engineering from IIT Bombay (8.6/10.0 GPA).

Long-Term Vision

AI, Robotics & the Future of Humanity

"My vision is a world where intelligent machines take on the dangerous, repetitive, and physically grueling work that currently costs people their health, time, and dignity — freeing them to be more human."

We are at the beginning of the most consequential technological transition in human history. Autonomous systems — robots that see, reason, and act — are crossing the threshold from controlled lab environments into the messy, unpredictable real world. This isn't about replacing humans. It's about designing the right systems for the right tasks: the warehouse worker whose back gives out at 45, the nurse spending four hours on logistics a robot could handle, the inspector walking through hazardous environments daily. These are the problems I want to solve — not as benchmarks, but as deployed, production-grade systems that actually reach people.

My long-term ambition is to work on robotic systems that operate in unstructured environments with human-level adaptability — in healthcare, disaster response, construction, and agriculture. The path there runs through better perception, smarter planning, safer human-robot interaction, and AI that is not just capable but trustworthy. I intend to spend my career on that path.
